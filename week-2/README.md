# Week 2: People Domain Deep Dive (42% of Exam)

## Why This Week Matters
The People domain accounts for **42% of the PMP exam** — that's approximately 75 out of 180 questions. This is the single largest domain and covers how you lead teams, manage stakeholders, resolve conflicts, and build high-performing project environments. Mastering this domain is the highest-ROI activity in your study plan.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this week, you should be able to:
1. Explain servant leadership and why PMI prefers it over other styles
2. Apply all 5 Thomas-Kilmann conflict resolution techniques and know PMI's preference order
3. Identify all 5 Tuckman stages and describe PM actions at each stage
4. Compare and apply motivation theories (Maslow, Herzberg, McGregor, McClelland, Vroom)
5. Use stakeholder engagement tools (Power/Interest grid, Salience model, engagement assessment matrix)
6. Describe the 5 components of emotional intelligence and their PM applications
7. Answer scenario-based exam questions using the PMI mindset for people management

## Key Themes for the People Domain
- **PMI loves servant leadership** — the PM removes obstacles, doesn't command
- **Collaborate/Problem-solve is the #1 conflict approach** — always try this first
- **Teams need psychological safety** — create environments where people can fail safely
- **Stakeholder engagement is proactive** — don't wait for problems, anticipate needs
- **Emotional intelligence > technical skills** — for leadership effectiveness on the exam
